Section 02
About This Project
Architectural rendering · Diez Mil Siete Arquitectura

Your home sits on Park Boulevard in San Juan, where two adjoining lots become a single fifteen-thousand-square-foot residence designed around the ocean view. Because you have lived in the existing structure, you already know how you want to move through it. That is a rare starting point, and it makes this the right moment to design: while the architecture is still taking shape with Alexandra and the team at Diez Mil Siete, the decisions that matter most are still open.

The current layout works against the way your family lives. It was built as two separate apartments, each with its own kitchen and a back entrance that was never meant to be the front door. The work ahead is to bring it together into one coherent home, one that functions for a growing family with a baby on the way and more planned, for older children who visit, and for the life you actually want to live in it, without losing what already works.

The aesthetic direction is earthy and layered, never monotone and never all beige. Olive and aubergine, warm woods, raw textures, a few unexpected pieces that hold a conversation. Tokyo meets Paris, grounded in the salt-air reality of San Juan, where materials have to earn their place. Teak, ipe, and cumaru over metals that rust in the sea air.

Our role is to design alongside your architect from the start, so that the way you actually live, where the children sleep, where the dogs drink, where the hammock hangs, is built into the bones of the house rather than solved after the fact.

Section 03
Scope by Room

Because the layout is still in development, the scope below reflects the spaces and intentions discussed to date. It will be refined as the floor plans are finalized with your architect.

Main Living Floor — Kitchen, Dining, and Lounge

This floor is where the family actually lives, and you have been clear it should read as one generous kitchen, dining, and lounge, not two competing living rooms. The challenge is making a single large space feel intentional rather than cavernous. We will design a layout where every zone has a real purpose: a kitchen that works as the gathering point, a dining area sized to seat the whole family at once, and a softer seating area for the moments after dinner when no one wants to leave the table.

The Hammock Moment

You raised the idea of a hammock or swinging chair in the open, view-facing space, and it is a smart instinct. Left as an afterthought it becomes clutter; designed properly it becomes the heart of the house. We will treat it as an architectural feature, built sturdy and deliberate, so it is the spot everyone gravitates to.

Children's Rooms and Nursery

The real problem here is time: two small bedrooms that serve as nurseries for a few years and then become dead space. Forest Garden and Interior Courtyard

The back bedrooms will not have the ocean view, which you flagged as a real limitation. Materials and Texture

The salt air rules out a lot, and you have lived with rust and mold on the wrong materials. We lean into raw, natural elements, layered textures, and salt-tolerant woods so the palette stays warm and organic while standing up to the island. Earthy, never flat.

Section 04
Design Fee

Your design fee is a flat fee. It covers the full creative scope of the project: planning, selections, specifications, sourcing and procurement of everything that goes into the home. There is no separate charge for purchasing. Our compensation on procurement comes through the items we source and sell.

The fee is calculated room by room, based on the work each space requires. Rooms that involve any element of construction, meaning anything beyond furniture, wallpaper, and accessories, are priced at $36 per square foot. Rooms that are furnishing and styling only are priced at $18 per square foot.

Project management, the hands-on coordination of the build and installation, is the one service billed separately by the hour. For this project we expect that to be minimal. You will be working closely with Brian Tenenbaum throughout construction, and our coordination role will layer in as the design phases move forward.

Because the layout is still in development with your architect, this fee is presented as an estimate. The final number depends on how many rooms involve construction versus furnishing and styling only, which is why it is shown as a range. It will be confirmed once the floor plans are final. With an architect of record already leading the construction drawings, the fee is likely to fall toward the lower end of the range.
