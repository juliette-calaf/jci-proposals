About This Project

The residence at 9700 West Broadview Drive is a single-story waterfront home in Bay Harbor Islands — a property with strong bones and a setting that invites an outdoor-centered way of living. Rather than rebuilding from the ground up, the decision to renovate within the existing footprint was a deliberate one, focused on creating something refined and enduring without starting over.

The design direction for this home is rooted in a Dorado, Balinese, and Tulum-inspired aesthetic: organic forms, natural materials, and a palette of green, white, and bronze that feels at home beside water. The result will be a home that breathes, shades beautifully, and wears well over time.

Outdoor living is a true priority here. The client entertains frequently and spends most of their time outside, so the exterior — two covered structures, the pool and deck, and the arrival sequence at the entry — will be treated with the same rigor and intentionality as the interior rooms. Every material selection will be made with durability and low maintenance in mind, including iguana-resistant landscaping and hurricane-compliant shading.

Interior scope centers on the main living areas, kitchen, primary suite, and guest rooms, with attention to ceiling heights, TV placement, and the overall flow from room to room. This is a home that will be lived in fully and entertaining often — and every decision we make will be grounded in that.

Scope by Room
Entry / Foyer

The arrival experience sets the tone for everything that follows. We will source an entry console, lighting, and curated objects that introduce the Balinese-Tulum direction immediately — layered, grounded, and quietly elevated. Water feature integration at the entry will be considered in close coordination with the exterior scope.

Living Room

The living room will be fully furnished and styled to anchor the home's social core. Seating, occasional tables, rugs, decorative lighting, and window treatments will all be selected in natural materials and a green, white, and bronze palette. The louvered wall solution and TV placement resolved during design development will inform the furniture layout from the start.

Dining Room

A statement dining table, chairs, and pendant lighting will be selected to hold their own while flowing naturally into the adjacent living and family spaces. Given the client's entertaining lifestyle, the dining room will be treated as a room that performs beautifully for both intimate dinners and larger gatherings.

Family Room

The family room will be furnished for daily living — comfortable, layered, and durable. Seating, soft goods, and decorative accessories will complement the reconfigured relationship between this room, the dining area, and the outdoor living spaces beyond. All selections will prioritize ease of maintenance without sacrificing quality.

Kitchen

Our Process
1
Conceptual Development
Study of existing conditions, space planning, mood boards, floor plans, and design concepts. Construction timeline and aesthetic direction established.
2
Design Development
Specification of all finishes, fixtures, furnishings, window coverings, decorative lighting, and custom millwork direction. First design presentation held.
3
Procurement
Purchasing proposals issued for client approval. No item ordered without a signed proposal and required payment. Vendor coordination and logistics managed by JCI.
4
Installation
Coordination of delivery, installation, and final styling. Site visits attended as needed and billed hourly under project management.

Estimated timeline varies based on scope. Full-scope projects with custom fabrication typically run 18 to 24 months. A detailed project schedule will be provided at kickoff.*
